Coding journey
I've always enjoyed a puzzle, and to me that's what coding is. During college, I took an Excel class as required part of my major where we learned VBA. Since then, I've poked around on codeacademy.com.

When I started with OpenDNS right after they were acquired by Cisco, I was on the recruiting team. I moved over to the customer success department to assist with onboarding new customers. I learned about DNS and how the internet works. We have Hackathons twice a year, and the first one I participated in I didn't have a project I was working on. One of the engineers taught a Python class. It was so much fun, I just wanted to keep learning.

The role I'm currently in doesn't offer much in the means of coding. It's a project based job, so sometimes I have downtime. I was curious one day about taking a bootcamp to learn to code. I googled coding bootcamps, and found a course through the University of Berkeley's extension program. It sounded perfect.

So far, I've gotten the chance to learn HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and jQuery. I've gotten to practice with many different and intersting projects, some featured on my portfolio page. Additionally, I got the experience of working in a group to build a really fun games app. I'm loving learning to code and would love to move into a role that allows for more coding and problem solving.
